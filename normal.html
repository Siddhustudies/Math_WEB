The normal distribution is one of the most important concepts in statistics. It describes how values of a variable are distributed. Most data in the real world ‚Äî from heights to test scores ‚Äî tends to follow this bell-shaped curve when measured at scale.

It is defined by its mean (average) and standard deviation (spread). As more and more data points are collected, the data often starts resembling a normal distribution due to the Central Limit Theorem.

üîç Why is it important?

  • Helps us calculate probabilities of events.
  • Used in standardizing tests like IGCSE, A-levels, SATs, etc.
  • Forms the foundation for confidence intervals and hypothesis testing.
  • Used in quality control, finance, insurance, machine learning, and more.

In practice:

  • Education: Exam scores from large populations (like IGCSE, A-levels) often follow a normal distribution, helping universities and exam boards set grade boundaries and percentiles.
  • Finance & Insurance: Stock returns, market risks, and claims data are often modeled using the normal distribution to calculate likelihoods and optimize decisions.
  • Manufacturing: Used in quality control ‚Äî e.g., if product weights deviate too much from the mean, they are flagged as defective.
  • Medical Science: Patient statistics like blood pressure, cholesterol, and even reaction times tend to follow normal patterns, guiding diagnosis and treatment thresholds.
  • Artificial Intelligence: Many machine learning algorithms assume data is normally distributed for better model performance and feature scaling.
